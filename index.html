About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Physics at the University of Calcutta, India. My research is broadly centered on nonequilibrium statistical physics, where I investigate how stochastic dynamics, environmental feedback, and resource constraints shape transport, search, and survival processes in complex systems. During my doctoral studies, I have worked on a variety of problems ranging from quantum walks and transport in disordered media to foraging dynamics, first-passage phenomena, and active matter.
A central theme of my research is understanding how the interplay between a moving agent and its environment gives rise to emergent behavior. To address these questions, I combine analytical theory, stochastic modelling, and numerical simulations to study systems in which the environment is not merely a passive background but evolves together with the dynamics of the particles, walkers, or foragers moving through it.
My work spans a wide range of systems, including quantum and classical random walks, transport in evolving and disordered environments, resource-dependent foraging models, active particles, and excitable media. Despite their apparent diversity, these systems share common questions about fluctuations, transport, exploration, and adaptation in nonequilibrium settings.
I am always interested in discussing new ideas and exploring potential collaborations. Please feel free to get in touch if you would like to learn more about my work or discuss possible research opportunities.
