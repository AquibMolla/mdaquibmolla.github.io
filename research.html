Research

Active Matter

This project explores whether coherent quantum particles can exhibit flocking and collective motion through local alignment interactions. By extending many-body quantum walks with interaction-driven coin dynamics, the research investigates how quantum interference, coherence, and correlations influence the emergence of collective transport. The goal is to establish a microscopic framework for quantum flocking, bridging concepts from active matter, nonequilibrium statistical physics, and quantum dynamics. The project further examines the robustness of collective quantum states under environmental effects and their connection to macroscopic descriptions of quantum active matter.

Forager Dynamics

My research focuses on stochastic foraging dynamics, exploring how survival strategies such as partial food consumption, energy thresholds, and intermittent resting influence the lifetime of a random-walking forager. Through numerical simulations and analytical approaches, I have uncovered scaling laws, crossover phenomena, and deviations from standard diffusive behavior that enhance forager survival. These studies demonstrate that strategic resource utilization and rest can significantly improve longevity beyond simple food availability considerations. This work provides a foundation for future investigations of adaptive foraging, resource regeneration, interacting foragers, and search processes in complex and heterogeneous environments.

Percolation Theory

Percolation phenomena in biological and quantum systems form the central focus of this work. In heart-like excitable media, I investigate how signal propagation, cellular switching dynamics, and network connectivity influence rhythm stability and conduction pathways. In correlated quantum materials such as 1T-TaS₂, I study pulse-induced phase transitions, charge-density-wave instabilities, and the emergence of fractal conductive networks. Through numerical simulations, scaling analysis, and theoretical modeling, this work reveals universal percolation characteristics across diverse systems. Future research will focus on non-equilibrium critical phenomena, adaptive and disordered networks, machine-learning-assisted phase identification, and transport dynamics in complex biological and quantum materials.

Quantum Walk

Quantum walks with dynamic measurement boundaries provide a versatile framework for understanding the interplay between quantum interference, transport, and observation. By introducing moving, periodically removed, and randomly relocated detectors, this work uncovers novel scaling laws, crossover phenomena, and occupation probability enhancements that arise purely from quantum effects. The results demonstrate how time-dependent measurements can profoundly modify quantum dynamics, leading to behaviors distinct from conventional infinite and semi-infinite quantum walks. These studies contribute to the broader understanding of monitored quantum systems and open avenues toward higher-dimensional quantum walks, disordered environments, quantum search protocols, and quantum information applications.
